When you arrive at the Richmond SPCA, you'll be asked to fill out an Adopter Survey. This one-page survey gathers information about your expectations of pet ownership and helps us determine which animals might best suit your lifestyle. Based on the results of the survey, you'll receive a color-coded Guest Pass to help guide you to cats or dogs that best meet your expectations.

Adoption Fee:

$110 per dog or cat of any age; when you adopt two pets on the same day, the fee is $200. Adoption fee includes spay or neuter procedure, age-appropriate vaccinations, free behavior helpline support, a packet of valuable discount coupons and special offers, and more! In addition, all pets adopted from the Richmond SPCA will be microchipped prior to adx. A microchip is inserted under your pet's skin and serves as a permanent form of identification. Registration with 24PetWatch for the first year is included. All Richmond SPCA alumni (and altered housemates) also qualify to become clients of the Clinic for Compassionate Care.

When Scheduled, Please Bring:
  • Picture ID with your current address (if current address not available, please bring current utility bill).
  • Proof of age (you must be 18 or older to adopt a pet from the Richmond SPCA).
  • Proper form of payment (cash, credit/debit card, money order, Richmond SPCA gift card). Checks are not an accepted form of payment.
  • A carrier for your adopted kitty or a leash and collar for your adopted dog. These and many more wonderful items for your new pet may also be purchased in our gift shop.

The money that you donate will go towards:
  • Spaying/Neutering the dog
  • Medical needs of the dog (rabies vaccinations, booster shots, heartworm preventative and flea preventative)

About Us

We are a non-profit organization dependent on donations.
Thank you for your support!

Angel Dogs is an all volunteer organization. We are a small organization that rescues dogs from local animal shelters and other places.

We typically keep around fifteen dogs at a time. We provide all necessary medical treatments including spaying or neutering, heartworm, and anything the veternarian recommends.

Once we rescue a dog, that dog will stay with us until he/she finds a good home.

We can use any type of assistance you can provide, such as volunteer dog handlers, foster homes for dogs, or donations of food or money.
